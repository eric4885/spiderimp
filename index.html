Objective

Clear the tableau by building eight same-suit sequences from King down to Ace. Each completed run moves to the Completed area. Finish all eight runs to win.

Setup

Spider uses two decks (104 cards) dealt into ten columns. At the start, 54 cards fill the tableau (some columns get six cards, others five); each column’s top card is face-up. The Deal pile sits at the top-left; completed runs collect at the top next to it. Choose difficulty before you play: 1 suit, 2 suits, or 4 suits.

When you move cards off a face-down card, it flips face-up automatically—exposing hidden cards early is a key tactic.

Valid moves

  • Place a card on the next-higher rank (suit does not matter for a single card).
  • Only a same-suit descending stack moves together as a group.
  • An empty column accepts any card or valid same-suit run.

Dealing from the stock

When every column has at least one card, click the Deal pile (top-left) to deal one card onto each column. You cannot deal while any column is empty—fill empties first. Empty columns are also why the rule exists: a deal would strand you with nowhere useful to park cards.
